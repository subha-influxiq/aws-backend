SD ba 6

Marker for angiotensin activity

Angiotensin is a peptide hormone that causes vasoconstriction and increase in blood pressure. It also stimulates the release of aldosterone from the adrenal cortex to promote sodium retention by the kidneys.

DPRS 2 & 5

Diastolic Pressure Response to Standing (DPRS) is the difference between the sitting diastolic minus the standing diastolic pressure. Normal <=5 ->=10 mmHg.

If above normal, additional cardio exercise may be recommended and possibly the need for additional Cardiometabolic testing.

ValsR 2 & 5

Valsalva Ratio (ValsR.) - is the measure of the Heart Rate response to Blood Pressure changes from the mechanical and cardiovascular effects of the Valsalva Maneuver.

If above normal, additional cardio exercise may be suggested after additional Cardiometabolic testing.
